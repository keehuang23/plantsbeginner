What is Philodendron Caramel Marble?

Philodendron caramel marbles are normal variegated plants that carry exotic shaded colors on leaves. There are various colors on these new leaves, ranging from caramel brown to dark forest green, which exactly looks like marble. In addition, these plants have very slow growth, and the size of leaves can grow even beyond your imagination. This philodendron caramel marble shows better growth in moist soil as compared to soggy soil. Proper natural sunlight through windows is required for the proper growth of these variegated plants, and water these plants daily in dry weather.

How to Care for Philodendron Caramel Marble

These beautiful and rare variegated plants are very expensive and hard to find, so taking care of these plants would be the topmost priority. These slow-growing plants need proper caring, and they need indirect sunlight because direct sunlight can harm these plants. High-quality potting mix and fertilizer should also be considered for quality growth in these plants. Soggy soil should be avoided while planting these philodendron caramel marbles. Do not water these plants unit they get dried. It can thrive in warm and humid weather.

Light

The philodendron caramel marble prefers to have indirect sunlight in medium or bright quantities. Sunlight is very important for the coloring of these leaves. It would be ideal to use natural light, but if you use artificial light, it would not be a problem. You will need fluorescent lighting or LED grow lights if your house doesn’t have enough windows or you want to keep these plants in the inner room of your house. Make sure that you have not put these philodendron caramel marbles in an area where the intensity of sunlight is very high. These plants are prone to high sunlight intensity.

Water

philodendron caramel marble does not need plenty of water; keep a proper difference while watering these plants because these variegated plants cannot tolerate the high amount of water. It is vital to leave the soil to dry before watering it again because these plants are susceptible to a high amount of moisture. 

Soil

The soil demand of philodendron caramel marble is mostly moist soil because they prefer a warm and humid environment. Soil that gets a high quantity of water every day becomes soggy and variegated plants do not prefer soggy soil.

Temperature

These variegated plants are capable of tolerating a wide temperature range. But the temperate that these plants prefer most is warm. The ideal temperature for these plants is 65 to 95 degrees Fahrenheit. These plants can be found in tropical forests on the south side of America. The weather in the tropical rainforest is warm and hot, varies from time to time, and also a lot of rainfall happens there. 

Humidity

Philodendron caramel marble needs high humidity for better development; humidity around 60 % to 80 % would be ideal for these plants. They can even survive in low humidity, around 40 %, but less than 40 % humidity can be critical for these variegated plants as low humidity makes these plants dry.

Fertilizers

In order to maintain the growth and colors of these philodendron caramels marble, you have to use some high-quality fertilizers which can sustain the growth and colors of these plants, but they do not require these fertilizers in high quantity. Do not feed your plants with a high quantity of nutrients because fertilizers contain slat, which can be toxic in high quality. 

Height and Growth of These Variegated 

Philodendron caramel marbles are slow-growing plants, but their leaves can grow larger beyond your imagination. Their leaves can reach up to 10 to 14 inches wide and 2 feet tall in height. 

Does Philodendron Caramel Marble Have Flowers?

No, these philodendron caramels marble plants do not contain any kind of flowers. However, the leaves of these variegate plants are much more beautiful than the flowers of ordinary plants because they often have more than two colors. They have diverse color options like pink, orange, and many more. 

How to Propagate Philodendron Caramel Marble?

These philodendron caramel marbles can be propagated from stem cutting. In addition, you can also divide the plants. The process of stem cuttings is very easy and straightforward; that’s why they are popular with home gardeners. You also get an option to propagate philodendron caramel marble directly into the water, or you can also plant these cuts directly into the soil. 

Step to propagate philodendron caramel marble through stem cuttings:

1. Select a healthy stem from these variegated and choose stems that have at least one node and few leaves on it

2. For cutting below these nods, you can use a sterile cutting tool because this tool ensures that the cut comes along with the cut. 

3. Now prepare a pot that you want to use to plant these philodendron caramel marbles and fill it with well-draining soil. 

4. Now apply to root hormones to the bottom end of the cutting, and after that, plant those nodes into the soil buried under.

5. Water those pots and put them in a brighter place, and provide indirect sunlight.

This process will take about one month for roots to develop, and after one month, roots will begin to establish themselves in the soil. Apart from the soil, you can also propagate them directly into the water. Take a container filled only with water to plant these nodes. Just ensure the nodes are submerged in the liquid. Those roots will take 3-4 weeks to develop. When roots become about 2 inches long, you can transfer them from the water pot into the potting mix. 

Philodendron Caramel Marble Repotting

The plant should be repotted every two years because repotting helps to provide the plant with fresh soil. While repotting, you must choose a pot larger than before because it would give philodendron caramel marble poisonous more space to grow. 

1. Gather all the materials which you are going to use in repotting; you will need a new pot, new fresh potting soil, and water. The new pot must be bigger than the older one to provide these plants space to grow. 

2. Gently pick up the sand from pots slowly until the whole sand is removed from the pot, and remove your plant in a safe manner so that no damage happens to the roots. 

3. Check the roots of your plants that they are still healthy or not, and if they are not, you must trim the unhealthy root before repotting them again.

4. After that, set up a new pot and fill it with new soil, and plant the roots of philodendron caramel marble in them; water them slowly; this is how repotting can be done.

Is Philodendron Caramel Marble Poisonous?

Philodendron caramel marble contains toxins such as calcium oxalate crystals, so yes, they are poisonous. These toxins are harmful when ingested. The poison present in these plants is only active when chewed, consumed, or swallowed. So if you have young children and pets like dot, cats, etc., you must place these variegated plants where your kids or pet cannot reach because it can be dangerous if they consume them.

Problems What Can Happen With Philodendron Caramel Marble

Pests

Pests are common problems for every plant, and pests can happen with variegated plants. However, this is not a major issue here, but still, it can be problematic sometimes. Plants come with natural pest-resisting qualities, but they resist only when they are fully healthy. So try to keep your philodendron caramel marble healthy and provide them a proper surviving environment. In contrast, these plants are prone to pests when they are not in a healthy condition. Some of the common bugs you may encounter with mealy bugs, spider mites, scales, and aphids. You can get rid of these pests with the help of neem oil and insecticidal soap. 

Diseases

Diseases can be caused in various parts of these plants. Some of the common diseases are root rot, leaf spot, and blight. Too much moisture can cause the root rot problem, and it can even kill your plant. The fungus can be responsible for leaf spot problems; leaf spots can turn the leaf of your plant yellow or eventually fall off. Blight can also kill your plant if you would not treat it as soon as possible; blight can be caused by bacteria. You must treat these diseases as soon as possible because they can completely damage your plant. Fungicide and bactericide would be ideal to use to treat these diseases. 

If you want to keep your philodendron caramel marble away from pests and diseases, follow these steps.

  • Keep the leafs dry of your plant 
  • Do not overwater these plants 
  • If you notice any sudden change in your plant, treat them immediately 

Leaves Are Turning Yellow and Brown

When you put your plant on the south facing, where most of the daylight comes, the sun is very harsh from late in the morning until mid-afternoon, and these variegation plants cannot tolerate that much intensity of light, and this can lead to leaves turning yellow. After some time, they will turn into brown color.

You can prevent the discoloration of your plants by keeping your plants 2 – 3 feet away from south-facing windows or any other window where sunlight is very harsh. Apart from this, you can filter the sunlight using sheer curtains because indirect sunlight does not affect philodendron caramel marble. 

NOTE: Do not try to put your plants away from the light because light is an essential element for the survival of these variegation plants. Lack of sunlight will cause them to fade away the color of their levees, and you will lose their vibrancy.

At the same time, the philodendron ring of fire has different colors even though they are also serrated, but they have a light green and light yellow color.

2. Size – The sizes of these philodendrons are very different; the philodendron marble grows around two feet tall, while the other grows so long, about 8 feet tall.

3. Price – The cost of these two verities don’t have some major difference philodendron ring of fire cost around $ 30 to $ 40 while marble cost around $ 20 to $ 30, ten dollars cheaper than the other.

4. Care – Both types of plants are easy to take care of, but still, there are a few major differences that you must know about. The philodendron rings of fire need water daily, and they need filtered light. On the other hand, philodendron caramels marble requires water once a week and needs indirect light.
